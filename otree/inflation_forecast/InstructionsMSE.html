Your score is your mean squared error (MSE) across the 18 scored years.

For each year, your squared error is:

Squared error = (Your forecast − Actual inflation)2

Your MSE is the average of these squared errors. Lower MSE = better forecasting.

Example

Suppose actual inflation turns out to be 4.0%.

Your forecast Error Squared error
6.0% (over by 2) +2.0 4.0
4.0% (exact) 0.0 0.0
2.0% (under by 2) −2.0 4.0

Notice that over-forecasting and under-forecasting by the same amount are penalized equally — squaring the error removes the sign. The only way to reduce your score is to forecast more accurately.
