Please read these instructions carefully before proceeding.

You are participating in a forecasting activity using historical data from an actual economy. The activity consists of 20 sequential years of data. Each year, you will be shown a table of macroeconomic indicators and asked to forecast the inflation rate for the following year. After submitting your forecast, you will immediately learn the actual outcome and how close your forecast was. You then move on to the next year in the sequence.

The data shown each year are:

  • Inflation rate (%)
  • Unemployment rate (%)
  • Federal funds rate (%)

Your task is to forecast the inflation rate for the upcoming year (in percent). Forecasts must be between –5.0% and 15.0%.

Please take the activity seriously and do your best to forecast accurately. Your effort helps make the results meaningful for the class discussion.

  • Years 1–2 are practice (unscored) — use them to get familiar with the interface.
  • Years 3–20 are scored. Your score is your mean squared error (MSE) — lower is better. MSE is explained on the next screen.
  • The total time limit is {{ time_limit_minutes }} minutes, starting from your first forecasting screen.
  • Grading: Your forecasting performance does not affect your grade. You receive full participation credit for completing the activity and reaching the final results screen.
  • If you get interrupted: do not close your browser. If you need to pause, leave the tab open and return to it. Reopening the link in a new tab will start you over from the beginning.
